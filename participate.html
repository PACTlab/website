Current study

Physiological profiling of age-related and noise-induced hearing loss

This study examines how different types of inner ear damage contribute to hearing difficulties in older adults. We are comparing the hearing profiles of younger adults with a history of significant noise exposure to older adults with minimal noise exposure. By measuring a battery of non-invasive tests, we aim to build a more precise picture of each person’s hearing loss and how it affects their ability to understand speech in noisy situations.

Who can participate?

  • Adults aged 45–75 years
  • Mild-to-moderate hearing loss (or normal hearing — both groups welcome)
  • No history of ear surgery or significant ear disease
  • Able to complete approximately 3 hours of testing over 1–2 visits

What does participation involve?

  • A standard hearing test (audiogram)
  • Non-invasive physiological measurements (no needles or discomfort)
  • Speech-in-noise testing and a brief hearing questionnaire
  • Compensation provided for your time
