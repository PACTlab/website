Samantha Hauser, AuD, PhD
Assistant Professor
Department of Communication Science and Disorders · University of Pittsburgh

Dr. Hauser is a hearing scientist and clinical audiologist whose research aims to connect the diverse peripheral pathologies underlying sensorineural hearing loss to individual differences in auditory perception, and to translate this knowledge into more precise diagnostic and treatment strategies. She established the Precision Audiology & Clinical Translation (PACT) Lab at Pitt in January 2026 following a postdoctoral position at Purdue University.

Her work uses a cross-species framework spanning human participants and preclinical animal models to develop and validate physiological biomarker batteries that go beyond the audiogram, grounding diagnosis in pathophysiology rather than audibility alone. Dr. Hauser is committed to training the next generation of clinician-scientists and co-founded the Audiology Clinician-Scientist Collective, a nationwide organization connecting AuD/PhD trainees and professionals.
